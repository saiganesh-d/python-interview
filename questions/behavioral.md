# Behavioral Question Bank — STAR stories

> Fractal is consulting-flavored: **communication & ownership matter as much as code.** Don't wing these — write them out.

## The STAR method
- **S**ituation — context (brief).
- **T**ask — your specific responsibility/goal.
- **A**ction — what *YOU* did (use "I", not "we"). The meat.
- **R**esult — outcome, ideally quantified ("cut load time 40%", "shipped 2 days early").

Keep each story ~60–90 seconds. Have numbers ready.

## ⭐ Other common questions (have a 1-liner ready)
- "Tell me about yourself." → 90-sec: who you are → key experience (Python/Django, deployed on AWS) → what you're great at → why this role. Practice this cold.
- "Why Fractal / why this role?" → Their AI/analytics + engineering blend; you want to build data-driven products full-stack. (Do 10 min of homework on Fractal before the interview.)
- "Your biggest weakness?" → Real but managed: e.g., "frontend depth — I'm strong backend, so I've been deliberately leveling up React" (then show you have). Avoid clichés.
- "Where do you see yourself in 3–5 years?" → Growing into a senior/lead engineer, deeper system design + mentoring.
- "Why leaving your current job / gap?" → Honest, forward-looking, no badmouthing.
- "A time you failed / made a mistake?" → Own it, show what you changed afterward.
- "How do you handle feedback / code review?" → Welcome it, ego-free, it improves the code.

## ⭐ Smart questions to ask THEM (shows seniority — prepare 3)
- "What does the engineering stack and deployment pipeline look like day to day?"
- "How are teams structured — do engineers own features end to end (backend + frontend + infra)?"
- "What does success in this role look like in the first 6 months?"
- "How does the team balance delivery speed with code quality / tech debt?"
- "What are the biggest technical challenges the team is facing right now?"

## Communication tips (Fractal weights this)
- Think out loud — they want to see your reasoning, not just the answer.
- If you don't know something: say "I haven't used X, but based on my understanding of Y, I'd approach it like..." — never bluff.
- Be concise. Answer the question, then offer to go deeper.
- Show ownership ("I drove...", "I decided...", "I'm responsible for...").
- Stay calm; it's a conversation, not an interrogation.
